# Subtext — Format Guide by Medium

## LinkedIn Longform (300–400 words)

**Structure:** Hook → Story → Insight → Implication → Takeaway. No labels. Narrative flow. Voice: confident, concrete, slightly irreverent.

**Example (Meta Glasses):**

Apple killed third-party tracking. Meta is betting your wrist can't compete with your eyeballs.

Meta's ad business has been dying since iOS 14 shut down IDFA (2021). Three years of rebuilding targeting through "behavioral signals"—what you click, what you buy—hasn't worked. Conversion rates on Instagram ads are half what they were. The problem: iOS won't let them see *who you are*, only what you do.

AR glasses solve this. They see where you look, what you linger on, who you're with, what store you just walked past. That data is 10x richer than anything iOS allows. Zuckerberg isn't betting on "computing." He's betting that the only way to rebuild their ad moat is to be the company trusted to wear a camera on your face all day.

Here's what's sharp: Apple can't block his access to your gaze. They blocked the app store. They blocked the device ID. But they can't block glasses from seeing what you look at. For the first time in 15 years, Meta has a data source Apple *can't* restrict.

Real-time visual AI is the gate. Without AI that instantly recognizes objects, places, intent—linking them together—the glasses are just a camera. With it, Meta owns a data flywheel: glasses capture intent → AI labels it → targeting gets sharper → advertisers pay more → reinvest in better AI. Apple has no answer because Siri can't compete with continuous visual context.

**The move:** The ad business rewrites itself when targeting moves from behavior to attention.

## Newsletter (500–700 words)

**Structure:** Longer narrative, can include context/color, "Why This Matters" closer, more intimate voice.

Same narrative as LinkedIn, but expand:
- Add more context on the original problem (IDFA killed how much targeting capacity?)
- Include a counter-argument or risk (privacy backlash, regulatory scrutiny)
- Expand "Why This Matters" section (who should care, implications)
- Can include a call-to-action or question for readers

## X Thread (5–7 tweets)

**Structure:** Tweet 1 = hook. Tweets 2–3 = story + decision. Tweets 4–5 = implication. Tweet 6 = takeaway. Each stands alone; all thread together. Casual, punchy, irreverent.

**Example (Meta Glasses):**

1/ Apple killed third-party tracking. Meta's answer? Bet that *your eyeballs* are better than your wrist.

2/ iOS 14 murdered IDFA. Meta spent 3 years trying to rebuild targeting through "behavioral signals" (what you click, what you buy). Conversion rates on Instagram ads are now *half* what they were. The problem: iOS won't let them see who you actually are.

3/ AR glasses fix this. They see where you look, what you linger on, who you're with, what store you just walked into. That data is 10x richer than anything iOS allows.

4/ Here's the move: Apple blocked the app store. Blocked the device ID. Can't block glasses from seeing what you look at. For the first time in 15 years, Meta has a data source Apple can't restrict.

5/ Real-time visual AI is the moat. Without it, just a camera. With it, Meta owns: glasses capture intent → AI labels it → targeting sharpens → advertisers pay more → reinvest in better AI. Apple has no answer.

6/ The ad business rewrites itself when targeting moves from behavior to attention.

## Short-Form Video Script (50–90 seconds)

**Structure:** Hook (0–3s) → Setup (3–20s) → Insight (20–45s) → Takeaway (45–50s). Punchy, visual cues, energy.

**Example (Meta Glasses):**

**[HOOK — 0–3s, high energy]**
"Apple killed Meta's ad business. Meta's response? Make you wear the camera."

**[SETUP — 3–20s, slower, explain]**
"When iOS 14 blocked third-party tracking, Meta lost access to *who you are*. They only see what you *do*. Conversion rates on Instagram ads? Cut in half. For three years, they've been trying to rebuild. It's not working."

**[INSIGHT — 20–45s, the actual move]**
"AR glasses change everything. They see where you look, who you're with, what store you walked into. That data is 10x richer than anything iOS allows. And here's the thing: Apple can't block your gaze. They can block apps. They can block device IDs. They *cannot* block glasses from seeing what you look at."

**[TAKEAWAY — 45–50s, punchy close]**
"The ad business rewrites itself when targeting moves from behavior to attention."

## General Rules Across All Formats

1. **No labels** (PS Take, AI Take, Self-Score). Strip them all. Integrate the insights into narrative.
2. **Open with the subtext, not the story.** Lead with the insight, then back it up.
3. **One-sentence takeaway.** Always ends clean, memorable, falsifiable.
4. **Skip the self-score.** Confidence lives in the writing, not in a label.
5. **Voice:** Confident, concrete, slightly irreverent. No buzzwords. No "in today's fast-paced world."
6. **Falsifiable > clever.** Would a PM read this and think "oh, that's why they did that"?
